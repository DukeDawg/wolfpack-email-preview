Shawnee Scream

The Long Hunter’s Journals 1

by B.N. Rundell

After a raid carried out by renegade warriors working for corrupt settlers destroys his family, apprentice gunsmith Jedediah Stroud heads into the Kentucky wilderness with grief at his back. As he tries to become a longhunter instead of just an avenger, the frontier forces him to choose between the man he hopes to be and the blood-soaked path of revenge.

Words from B.N. Rundell

After a milestone season—my wife and I celebrating 60 years of marriage, both of us turning 81, and moving from Colorado to Kentucky—I’m excited to launch Shawnee Scream, the first novel in The Longhunter’s Journals. Set on the mid-1700s Kentucke frontier, this new series follows different characters as I explore the land, its history, and its dangers. I hope you’ll ride with me.

Under Heaven's Own Blue

by John D. Nesbitt

Four classic Wyoming frontier stories follow men and women chasing the truth through grief, fraud, missing girls, buried crimes, and powerful enemies. In John D. Nesbitt’s wide-open country, secrets have nowhere to hide—and justice is still worth the ride.

Words from John D. Nesbitt

This collection brings together one short story and three novellas in the western-mystery crossover mode I enjoy—stories of principle, deceit, murder, missing girls, and frontier secrets. Field trips through Goshen Hole and the Pumpkin Buttes helped me shape the settings, and I’m glad to have these middle-length works together in one volume.
