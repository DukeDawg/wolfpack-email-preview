Night Passage

by Norman A. Fox

Norman A. Fox's Night Passage is the classic western that inspired the James Stewart film. Five years ago, one bad night stripped Grant McLain of his name, his railroad job, and the life he wanted. Now he drifts the Montana frontier playing accordion in saloons — the last man any outfit would trust with ten thousand dollars in payroll, which is exactly why they ask him. Curly Harbin's gang has robbed the railroad blind three times running, with an inside man feeding them every move. One night, one train ride into the Crazy Mountains, and Harbin already knows McLain is coming.

Summer's Lease

by John D. Nesbitt

John D. Nesbitt's Summer's Lease is a frontier mystery with a quiet, deadly edge. Lawrence Orme came to Wyoming to rebuild a life that had come apart, but he finds a body on his doorstep before he's even unpacked — a warning to stop asking questions better left unasked. When a decent neighbor running for local office turns up garroted in front of the whole town, Orme realizes there are old crimes buried in this corner of Wyoming, and the man who buried them won't let a soft-spoken newcomer dig them up.
